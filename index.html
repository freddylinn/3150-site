About Me

Hi! I'm Freddy. I'm a junior in computer science at CU Boulder. I enjoy all kinds of programming, but have mainly focused on web development. This makes me excited to learn universal design so that I can make my websites more accessible and a better experience for everyone that uses them. I am also part of a web development club here at CU called Blueprint Boulder so I hope to apply this knowledge towards all of our annual projects as well as the club website. Outside of classes, I enjoy climbing, skiing, surfing, and anything else outdoors.

So far I've learned a lot more about accessible web design. This includes labeling sections, using semantic HTML for the correct purposes, and more. This has helped me learn fundamental concepts that I can apply in all future projects. It has also given me the opportunity to tell teammates and organizations about these best practices in hopes of making the web more accessible. HTML5 is a powerful tool that can make navigating websites much easier for all users.

My Favorite Projects

One of my favorite projects at CU Boulder was in my Intro to Cybersecurity class. For this project, we were tasked with testing student sites from the intro to web development course for vulnerabilities. This gave me the opportunity to apply the skills I'd learned in a controlled environment and brainstorm how any vulnerabilities could be patched. It felt like a learning experience for both myself and the students whose website I tested, so I liked how it was mutually beneficial. It also felt close to a real world penetration testing scenario.
